About the writer

A Mind in Reflection

I am not an expert. I have no credentials to wave, no framework to sell. I am simply someone who pays attention — to the way light falls through a window at different hours, to the hesitation in someone's voice when they're about to lie, to the strange peace that comes after crying.

This space exists because I've learned that thoughts unrecorded are thoughts lost. And in a world that moves too fast to think, I've found that the act of writing itself is a form of resistance — a small, quiet rebellion against the noise.

Here, I write about life as I experience it: messy, uncertain, often contradictory. I don't have answers. I have questions worth sitting with. And if you've found your way here, perhaps we share the same hunger — for meaning that doesn't come pre-packaged.

— Ritish
